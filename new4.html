About Us

Despite global efforts, oceans face threats from climate change, habitat destruction, and overfishing, endangering biodiversity and livelihoods. Limited resources and declining quality of life for coastal communities worsen as fishing stocks dwindle, impacting income and cultural identity. Marine degradation diminishes tourism and clean water access, exacerbating poverty. Loss of traditional practices erodes cultural resilience, further straining communities. Global action is imperative to mitigate climate change, promote sustainable fisheries, and support coastal adaptation.
